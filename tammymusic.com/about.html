Biography

Rated as one of the best female artists in Zimbabwe, Tamy, real name Thamsanqa Moyo is a 19-year-old Afro Pop fusion singer, song writer and guitarist who successfully launched her Debut European tour in 2017.
Having started her musical journey at a tender age of 7, singing at school functions weddings cooperate and embassy events, Tamy showed she was a force to reckon with as she performed at the Madison Square garden in New York at this young age. She then spent the greater part of her high school career holding the office of Childline Ambassador, a role that saw her being the voice of the youth and children in Zimbabwe.
During her tenure, she penned the childline theme song as well as many other campaigns amongst them being 'say no to child abuse', 'Zero tolerance to child abuse', 'The commemoration of the African child and 'The commemoration of the rights of the children'. The talented singer then announced her arrival on the professional music scene with her single 'Sango', a 2015 production that welcomed her as a rare find and a breath of new air to the music scene as she demonstrated depth in her songwriting and uniqueness in her musicality with an overall appreciation of her gift.
For her 18th birthday, Tamy celebrated the milestone by launching her debut album titled 'The 18th Roller Coaster', which was highly rated by music critics and fans. The first track 'Ndibereke' on the album became vastly appreciated across Zimbabwe and in the diaspora where it got an overwhelming response attracting good rotation and airplay on various radio stations.

Achievements

The NAMA (National Arts Merit Awards) Nominee in the best female category 2016, ZIMBA Nice on­line Best Female Afro Jazz Artist and Best Afro Jazz Album, Tamy aka Thamsanqa Moyo has found her space on the Zimbabwean Music Landscape. The launch of her Debut Album the 18 Rollercoaster in the year 2016 marked her entrance on the big stage as by the day she gained favour and popularity both from the music loving fans as well as all local radio stations.
Tamy, is a singer song writer and left hand guitarist who was born on the 5th of January 1 998, grew up in Harare, Zimbabwe. She did her Elementary school at Lusitania Primary School from Grade 1 to Grade 7 that is from 2004 to 201 0 it was during this period that her musical gifting was discovered at the age of 7. At a tender age she began a perfomance circuit that included Weddings, School Events, National Functions such as National Arts Merit Award Ceremony and other corporate engagements. If talent and passion can take one far Tamy is the perfect example of such a gift that is destined to take the world by storm. Her Vision and Mission for 201 7 is to conquer the International market.

AT THE AGE OF 8 (2006) Tamy began to perform at a number of local plat­forms such as CBZ ‘A’ academy, National Arts Merit Awards and HIFA (Harare In­ternational Festival of the Arts)

AT THE AGE OF 9 (2007) Tamy went on to join the Africa children's choir in 2009 at the Madison Square Garden, New York USA under Methodist Church initiative. She also participated in the HIFA opening night with local artist such as Chiwoniso Maraire and Prudence Katomeni.

AT THE AGE OF 10 (2008) Tamy performs with the music legend Oliver Mtukudzi at the Winter Jazz festival at Africa University in Mutare together with Dudu Manhenga.
JOE THOMAS IN ZIMBABWE She got an opportunity to perform with All Time American RNB Legend Joe Thomas the hit song "I wanna know".

AT THE AGE OF 12 (2010) Tamy began to embark on song writing and had her first solo act at HIFA 2010 performing at the Youth Zone.
AT THE AGE OF 13 (2011) Tamy went on to perform at the launch of SAFAIDS. This year Tamy launched her first 2 singles Celebrate your Life and African beat, a project done by David Sengwayo and Monomukundu. Due to her musicality and the impact of her songs in 2011, she was appointed the Child Line Ambassador. This role saw her working on a number of National Cam­paigns advocating for the rights of the children such as the Commemoration of the Children Rights, Say No to child abuse & Zero tolerance to child abuse.

AT THE AGE OF 14 (2012) NATIONAL CAMPAIGN THEME SONGS as the Child line am­bassador Tamy wrote the theme songo 11 6 to encourage children in difficult situations to call out for help. Through the Ministry of Health ad Child Warfare she penned and performed a theme song entitled Zero tolerance to child abuse. It became evident that Tamy's song writing, singing and perfomance skills were in a class of her own that she was invited once again to write the theme song for commemoration of the children rights convection under the theme "I am the future, I am the future."

SING YOUR WAY TO PARIS COMPETITION She participate in the Alliance Francaise initiative "sing your way to Paris" as the only non-french speaking contestant and she took 3rd place. 201 2 HIFA could not resist this young dynamic energetic talent that was making waves in the Zimbabwe Arts Industry and so it fit to give her yet another opportunity to be part of the Hifa opening ceremo­ny perfomance directed by V.
